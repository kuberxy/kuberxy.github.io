介词之for

todo

1、给、对 [表示对象或用途]

used to say who is intenede to get or use something, or where something is intended to be used

例句：

  • I’ve got a present for you

    我有件礼物送给你

  • Someone left a message for Vicky

    有人给维基留了言

  • An English course for foreign students

    为外国学生开的一门英语课程

  • We need a new battery for the radio

    我们的收音机需要一节新电池

  • These chairs are for the office

    这些椅子是办公室的

2、为了、以帮助

in order to help someone or something

例句：

  • I looked after the kids for them

    我为他们照看孩子

  • Let me carry that bag for you

    我来给你拿那个包吧

  • The doctor knew that there was nothing he cloud do for her

    医生知道自己无能为力，帮不了她

  • Charles died fighting for his country

    查尔斯在战斗中为国捐躯

  • What can I do for you?

    = used to ask a customer if you can help them?

    我可以帮您做点什么吗？

3、为了、供 [表示目的]

used to say what the purpose of an object, action etc is

例句：

  • Knife for cutting bread

    切面包的刀子

  • What did you do that for?

    你那样做是为了什么？

  • I’ve bought him a watch for his birthday

    我给他买了块手表作为生日礼物

  • The documents prepared for his defence

    为给他辩护而准备的文件

4、为了要；为了做；为了得到

in order to have, do, get, or obtain something

例句：

  • Are you waiting for the bus?

    你在等公共汽车吗？

  • the qualifications necessary for a careerin broadcasting

    从事广播业必须具备的资格

  • Mother was too ill to get up for a dinner

    母亲病得无法起床吃晚饭

  • I paid $3 for a ticket

    我花三美元买了张票

  • For further details, write to this address

    欲知详情，请致函此地址

  • Let’s go for a walk

    我们去散步吧

5、达，计 [表示动作或情况持续的时间]

used to say how long an action or situation continues for

例句：

  • Bake the cake for 40 minutes

    将蛋糕烘烤40分钟

  • Wh had been talking for a good half hour

    我们谈了足足半小时

  • He’s been off work for a while

6、达、计 [表示距离]

used to talk about distance

例句：

  • We walked for miles

    我们走了好几英里

  • Factories stretch for quite a way along the canal

    工厂沿运河延伸了很长一段距离

7、在…时间 [表示安排]

if something is arranged for a particular time, it is planned that it should happen then

例句：

  • I’ve invited them for 9 o’clock

    我邀请他们9点来

  • A meeting was arranged for 18th May

    我有一个会议安排在5月18日

8、往、向 [表示去向]

used to say where a person, vehicle etc is going

例句：

  • I set off for work

    我动身去上班

  • the train for Manchester

    开往曼彻斯特的列车

  • A few days later she would be leaving for New York

    过几天她就要去纽约了

9、达、计 [表示价格或价值]

used to say what the price or value of something is

例句：

  • a cheque for a hundred pounds

    一张100英镑的支票

  • The diamond was insured for two thousand dollars

    这颗钻石上了2000美元的保险

10、由于、因为

because of or a result of something

例句：

  • If, for any reason, you cannot attend, please inform us

    如因故不能出席，请通知我们

  • We could hardly see for the mist

    由于雾气，我们几乎什么也看不见

  • You’ll feel better for a break

    休息一下你会感觉好些

  • a reward for making good progress

    对取得良好进步的奖赏

  • Campbell was arrested for dangerous driving

    坎贝尔因危险驾车而遭逮捕

11、对于 [表示陈述或问题的对象]

used to say which thing or person your statement or question is related to

例句：

  • I’m sure she’s the ideal person for the job

    我确信她是这个职位最理想的人选

  • The questiosn on this paper are too difficult for 10-years-olds

    这张试卷上的题目对于10岁的孩子来说太难了

  • Are you all right for money?

    钱你没有问题吧？

  • Fortunately for him, he can swim

    好在他会游泳

12、对于 [表示情感表露的对象]

used to say which person or thing your feeling are directed towards

例句：

  • I came away feeling sorry for poor old George

    我走的时候，心里为可怜的老乔治感到难过

  • My deep love for him still remains

    我对他深爱依旧

  • They show no respect for authority

    他们无视权威

13、当作 [表示食物在哪一餐吃]

used to say at which meal you eat something

例句：

  • We had pasta for lunch

    我们午餐吃了意大利面

14、受雇于、效力于

used to say which company, team etc you belong to

例句：

  • I’ve worked for the BBC ever since I left university

    我大学毕业后一直在英国广播公司工作

  • Deborah used to play for the A team

    德博拉增效力于A队

  • He writes fro a weekly paper

    他为一家周报撰稿

15、支持、拥护、赞同

supporting or in agreement with something or someone

例句：

  • We have studied the arguments for and against nuclear energy

    我们研究了支持和反对核能的论点

  • How many people voted for the proposal?

    有多少人投票支持这项提议？

  • Three sheels for the captain

    为队长欢呼三声

  • I’m all for giving people more freedom

    我完全赞成给予民众更多的自由

16、意思是，词语或标志表示

used to say what a word or sign means

例句：

  • What’s the French word for ‘happy’?

    法语“开心”怎么讲

  • Red is for danger

    红色表示危险

17、就…而言 [表示某种特点令人惊讶]

used to say that a particular quality of someone or something is surprising when you consider what the are

例句：

  • She looks young for her age

    就她的年龄而言，她看上去算是年轻的

  • It’s cold for July

    就7月份来说，这天气算是冷的

18、代表

as a representative of other people

例句：

  • Paisley claims to speak for the majority of local people

    佩斯利声称自己是当地大部分民众的喉舌

19、对 [某人] 来说 [可能、困难、必须、不寻常等]

used to say what is possible, difficult, necessary unusual etc

例句：

  • It’s unusual for Donald to be so bad-tempered
  • There is an urgent need for someone to tackle thie problem
  • Here is a chance fo everyone to learn new skills
  • There’s nothing worse than for a parent to ill-treat a child
  • It was too far for her to walk in high-heeled shoes

20、每…就有

for each / every used to say that there is a relationship between one amount and another

例句：

  • For each mistake, you’ll half a point

    你每错一处，就要扣去半分

  • For every threee people who agree, you’ll find five who don’t

    每三个人同一，就有五个人不同意

21、某事物不合某人之意

sth is not for sb

used to say that something is not the kind of thing that someone likes or will enjoy

例句：

  • City life is not for me

    城市生活不适合我

  • This book is not for everyone

    这本书不是所有人都会喜欢的

22、（不）应该由某人来做某事

it is (not) for sb to do sth

used to say whether it is someone’s right or duty to do something

例句：

  • It’s not for me to tell you what to do

    不应该由我来告诉你怎么办

  • It will be for you to decide what action you should take

    你应该采取什么行动由你自己来决定

23、要不是某人/某物

if it wasn’t / weren’t fo sb / sth

= if it hadn’t been for sb / sth

used to say who or what prevents or prevented something from happening

例句：

  • If it hadn’t been for you, I should have drowned

    要不是你的话，我就淹死了

24、那正是某人/某物的特点 [尤用于表示某人或某物不可能有多好]

that’s / there’s sb / sth for you [口语]

used to say that a particular kind of behaviour or situation is typical of someone or something, especially when you do not expect anything better from that person or thing

  • I know it’s outrageous, but that’s Melissa for you

    我知道这很离谱，但梅利莎就是这么个人

25、那还能算是…？那恰好跟…相反！

that’s / there’s sb / sth for you [口语]

used when you are annoyed or disappointed to say that something is the opposite of the quality you are mentioning

例句：

  • Well, there’s gratitude for you! Here am I trying to help and you tell me not to interfere

    嘿，哪有这样表示感谢的！我来帮忙，你却叫我别插手

26、要挨骂，要受罚

be (in) for it [口语]

to be likely to be blamed or punished

例句：

  • You’ll be in for it if she finds out what you’ve done

    要是她发现你干的事，你就麻烦了
